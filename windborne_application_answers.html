1. Semantle Strategy Guide

Understanding Semantle: Semantle is a word guessing game where you try to find a secret word. Unlike traditional word games, you don't get told if letters are correct—instead, you get a similarity score (0-100) that represents how semantically similar your guess is to the secret word based on word embeddings.

Strategy for Beginners:

  • Start Broad: Begin with common, general words across different categories (e.g., "animal", "food", "emotion", "action") to establish a baseline and understand the semantic space.
  • Similarity Score: Higher scores mean you're closer semantically. If you get a score above 50, you're in the right conceptual neighborhood—try synonyms, related concepts, or words in the same category.
  • Think Conceptually: The game uses semantic similarity, not spelling. "Happy" and "joyful" are closer than "happy" and "happen", even though the latter shares more letters.
  • Use Hints Strategically: Don't be afraid to use hints. They can reveal words with high similarity scores, giving you anchor points to explore from.
  • Narrow Down: Once you have several high-scoring words, identify the common theme or category they share, then explore more specific terms within that domain.
